Thornwall - Settlement Lore
Region: Erimentha
Classification: Small Town / Adventurer's Outpost
Status: Peaceful, low-threat

Overview
Thornwall is a small but resilient settlement on the edge of the Ashfield, a wide expanse of grey-dusted plains to the east. The town is modest by most measures, home to a handful of permanent residents, a working blacksmith, a well-kept inn, and a local chapter of the Monster Hunters Guild. Despite its quiet present, Thornwall carries the weight of a dramatic past, and the evidence of that past is written into the land itself.

History
The Age of Ash
Long before Thornwall became the welcoming stopover it is today, the region was considered uninhabitable. The Ashfield to the east was not always so named for its grey soil alone. For generations, a population of dragons claimed the skies above the plains, their presence reducing the surrounding land to scorched earth and drifting cinders. Settlements attempted to take root in the area multiple times over the centuries, but none survived longer than a season. The ash that still blankets the Ashfield is a remnant of that era, a quiet geological memory of fire and ruin baked into the soil so deeply that even centuries of wind and rain have not fully undone it.
The dragons are not a recent threat. No living person in Thornwall has seen one, and their great-grandparents likely did not either. They exist in the town's identity as myth and explanation, the reason the land looks the way it does, the reason the town was built the way it was.
The Guild's Arrival
The turning point in Thornwall's history came when the Monster Hunters Guild established a presence in the region and began issuing bounties on the dragons and the various dangerous creatures that had flourished under their shadow. Adventurers came from distant parts of Erimentha, drawn by the promise of significant coin and the chance to make a name for themselves. Over time, through coordinated hunts and attrition, the dragon population was driven out or destroyed. The monsters that had gathered in their wake scattered or were cleared out alongside them.
What was left behind was a town that had grown up around the effort. Supply routes, temporary lodgings, a smithy to repair weapons and armor, all of it slowly became permanent. Thornwall had earned its name from the thick thorn-brush barriers the early settlers used to mark safe perimeters during the worst years. The name stuck even after the walls were no longer needed.

Present Day
Thornwall today is quiet, almost sleepily so. The Monster Hunters Guild hall still stands near the center of town and still posts bounties, though they are rarely dramatic. The most common contract work involves culling the local slime populations that gather in the damp hollows around the Ashfield's edge. It is not glamorous work, but it pays reliably and gives newer adventurers somewhere to begin.
The inn serves as the social heart of the town, a place where travelers rest, locals trade gossip, and the occasional adventurer nurses a bruised ego after their first slime encounter. The blacksmith handles repairs, basic commissions, and the resale of salvaged gear, catering primarily to the steady trickle of people passing through rather than any large-scale trade.
Thornwall has a reputation as a starting point. Experienced hunters rarely linger long. The town seems to understand its role and wears it without resentment, proud of a history that made it, and comfortable in a present that serves others well enough.

The Ashfield
The plains east of Thornwall stretch wide and flat, their soil a pale, powdery grey that gives the region its name. Nothing dramatic happens in the Ashfield anymore. The ash does not fall fresh, the sky is not darkened, and the ground is not hot underfoot. What remains is a landscape that has simply never fully recovered, dotted with scrubby vegetation that tolerates poor soil and slimes that thrive in the soft, damp depressions where ash has settled deepest. On overcast days, when the light is flat and the grey ground meets a grey sky, the Ashfield can feel genuinely eerie. Locals are used to it. New arrivals rarely are.

Role in the World
Thornwall serves as a natural entry point for adventurers beginning their careers. The Monster Hunters Guild presence provides structure, the low-threat bounty environment provides income without excessive risk, and the town's history provides context that makes even a slime contract feel like part of something larger. It is the kind of place people leave when they are ready for more, but remember fondly when they are far from home.
